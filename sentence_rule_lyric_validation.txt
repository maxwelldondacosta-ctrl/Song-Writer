THE SENTENCE RULE Song Writing Project – Lyric Integrity Check

  ----------
  OVERVIEW
  ----------

The Sentence Rule is a simple validation method used to ensure that
lyrics retain meaning and narrative coherence during editing or
optimization.

Many lyric editing systems focus on rhyme, syllable counts, or pattern
matching first. This often results in lines that technically fit rhythm
but no longer make logical or emotional sense.

The Sentence Rule prevents this problem by ensuring that meaning and
context are preserved before any technical adjustments are made.

  ----------------
  CORE PRINCIPLE
  ----------------

A lyric must always make sense as a sentence and within the surrounding
lyrical context.

If a line fails the meaning test, it must be rewritten or discarded
regardless of rhyme or rhythm quality.

  -----------------
  THE FOUR CHECKS
  -----------------

Every lyric line must pass the following four checks.

1.  Sentence Logic

Does the line make sense by itself as a sentence?

Example failure: “Coke and liquor numb my blood”

Blood cannot be numbed, therefore the sentence fails logical meaning.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.  Context Continuity

Does the line make sense with the line before and after it?

Lyrics should form a coherent progression of ideas rather than isolated
statements.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.  Narrative Consistency

Does the line support the emotional theme or story of the verse or song?

Each line should reinforce the core emotional idea rather than introduce
unrelated imagery.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.  Singability

Even if a line is logically correct, it must also fit comfortably within
a melody.

Check for:

• awkward phrasing • unnatural word stress • difficult vocal rhythm

If a line is difficult to sing, it may require minor adjustments while
preserving meaning.

  --------------------
  VALIDATION PROCESS
  --------------------

For each lyric line:

1.  Check sentence meaning
2.  Check contextual connection
3.  Check narrative alignment
4.  Check melodic singability

If all four checks pass, the line should remain unchanged.

If any check fails, revise the line while preserving the original
emotional intent.

  ----------
  KEY RULE
  ----------

Meaning always takes priority over rhyme, syllable count, or pattern
optimization.

A technically perfect lyric that makes no sense is still a failed lyric.
