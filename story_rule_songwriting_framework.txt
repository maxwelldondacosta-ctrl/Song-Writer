THE STORY RULE Song Writing Project – Narrative Impact Framework

  ----------
  OVERVIEW
  ----------

The Story Rule defines how emotional storytelling should function inside
song lyrics.

Unlike novels or films, songs have limited space to describe scenes or
complex narratives. Lyrics therefore rely on compressed storytelling.

Effective songs usually communicate three essential elements:

1.  What happened
2.  How it made the narrator feel
3.  What the narrator will do about it

If a song includes all three elements, it tends to feel emotionally
complete and satisfying to the listener.

  ----------------
  CORE PRINCIPLE
  ----------------

A song must communicate a clear emotional narrative within a limited
number of lines.

Lyrics should focus on the event, the emotional reaction, and the
resulting decision or consequence.

  ----------------------------
  THE THREE STORY COMPONENTS
  ----------------------------

1.  EVENT (The Trigger)

Something happens that initiates the story.

Examples:

You left You lied You betrayed me

The event establishes the cause of the emotional response.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.  EMOTIONAL REACTION

The narrator expresses the emotional effect of the event.

Examples:

Now I can’t breathe Every night I break Your ghost still haunts me

This stage creates emotional relatability and depth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.  RESOLUTION OR DIRECTION

The narrator reaches a conclusion or makes a decision.

Examples:

I’ll burn it down I’m letting you go I’ll never trust again

This gives the song closure, escalation, or transformation.

  ----------------------------
  WHY THE STORY RULE MATTERS
  ----------------------------

Songs that miss one of these components often feel incomplete.

Missing Event: The story becomes confusing.

Missing Emotion: The song feels emotionally flat.

Missing Resolution: The ending feels unsatisfying or unresolved.

Many weak lyrics repeat emotional statements without explaining the
cause or the outcome.

  ------------------------
  COMMON FAILURE PATTERN
  ------------------------

Emotion without context:

“I’m sad I’m broken I’m crying I’m lost”

Without an event or resolution, these statements lack narrative impact.

  --------------------------------------
  INTEGRATION WITH OTHER PROJECT RULES
  --------------------------------------

The Story Rule operates alongside other lyric validation systems.

Sentence Rule: Ensures each lyric line maintains logical meaning.

Pattern Libraries: Ensure the lyric structure fits genre performance
needs.

Prompt Framework: Ensures the generated track matches the intended
style.

Together these systems ensure that lyrics are:

• meaningful • coherent • structurally effective • emotionally impactful

  ----------
  KEY RULE
  ----------

Lyrics should tell a clear emotional story, even when the language is
symbolic or poetic.

Listeners must be able to understand:

what happened, why it mattered, and what the narrator feels or decides.
