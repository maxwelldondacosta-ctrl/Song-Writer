THE PHONETIC TEXTURE RULE Song Writing Project – Sound and Word Impact
Framework

  ----------
  OVERVIEW
  ----------

The Phonetic Texture Rule governs how the sound of words affects the
emotional tone of song lyrics.

In music, words are not only meaning — they are sound. Listeners react
to the phonetic texture of a line as much as its literal meaning.

Certain consonants and vowel shapes create different emotional
impressions. Because of this, vocabulary should be intentionally limited
to words that support the desired emotional tone of the song.

This rule prevents lyrics from using technically correct words that
sound wrong for the mood of the track.

  ----------------
  CORE PRINCIPLE
  ----------------

The sound of a word must support the emotional tone of the lyric.

If the phonetic texture clashes with the intended emotion, the word
should be replaced.

  ------------------
  CONSONANT IMPACT
  ------------------

Hard consonants create sharper, more aggressive sounds.

Examples: K, T, P, D, G

These sounds feel percussive and are often used in aggressive or
energetic music.

Example words: crack cut break punch

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Soft consonants create smoother, gentler sounds.

Examples: M, N, L, W, Y, B

These sounds flow more easily and often appear in romantic or emotional
lyrics.

Example words: love mellow baby alone

  ---------------------
  FIRST SYLLABLE RULE
  ---------------------

The first syllable of a word determines the emotional attack of the
line.

A sharp opening sound can create tension or aggression.

Examples: break cut kill crash

A soft opening sound creates warmth or tenderness.

Examples: maybe baby love tender

  -----------------------
  VOCABULARY LIMITATION
  -----------------------

Songwriting benefits from a restricted vocabulary aligned with the
emotional tone of the song.

Examples:

Anger / aggression: burn break crush kill fight

Gentle / emotional: love hold baby tender heart

Dark / despair: void grave shadow dust night

Using vocabulary that matches the emotional tone strengthens the sonic
coherence of the lyric.

  -----------------------
  WHY THIS RULE MATTERS
  -----------------------

Many weak lyrics fail because the word sounds clash with the intended
emotion.

Example:

An aggressive song using soft romantic vocabulary will sound weak.

A tender song using harsh percussive words will sound unnatural.

Matching phonetic texture with emotional tone keeps the lyric sonically
consistent.

  ------------------------------
  INTEGRATION WITH OTHER RULES
  ------------------------------

Sentence Rule: Ensures each lyric line makes logical sense.

Story Rule: Ensures the song communicates an emotional narrative.

Pattern Library: Ensures the lyric structure fits the musical genre.

Phonetic Texture Rule: Ensures the words themselves sound correct for
the emotion and delivery of the song.

  ----------
  KEY RULE
  ----------

The meaning of the word matters, but the sound of the word must match
the emotion.

Good lyrics choose words that feel right when spoken or sung.
