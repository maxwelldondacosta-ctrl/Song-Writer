CADENCE PATTERN LIBRARY Song Writing Project – Vocal Rhythm Framework

  ---------
  PURPOSE
  ---------
 
This document defines common vocal cadence patterns used across major
music genres. Cadence determines how lyrics are rhythmically delivered
over a beat.

Two lyrics with identical rhyme schemes can feel completely different
depending on cadence. This library defines repeatable rhythmic patterns
that appear in successful songs.

Cadence patterns should be selected before rhyme optimization.

  ----------------
  CORE PRINCIPLE
  ----------------

Cadence controls:

• stress placement • syllable grouping • rhythmic subdivision • breath
spacing

If cadence is wrong, even strong lyrics will perform poorly.

  ----------------------------
  1. STRAIGHT 4‑BEAT CADENCE
  ----------------------------

Structure:

DA da DA da DA da DA da

Example structure:

I WALK through FIRE I BREAK the CHAINS

Characteristics:

• strong beat alignment • predictable rhythm • powerful emphasis points

Common genres:

• rock • metal • pop choruses • stadium anthems

Observed in many heavy chorus structures and chant-style hooks.

  ----------------------------
  2. DOUBLE‑TIME RAP CADENCE
  ----------------------------

Structure:

da‑da‑da‑da DA da‑da‑da‑da DA

Example structure:

Running through the city with a vision never stopping Everybody talking
but they never really popping

Characteristics:

• high syllable density • fast delivery • momentum driven

Common genres:

• grime • technical rap • fast hip‑hop verses

  --------------------
  3. TRIPLET CADENCE
  --------------------

Structure:

DA‑da‑da DA‑da‑da

Example structure:

Money on my mind Running all the time

Characteristics:

• rolling rhythmic feel • hypnotic flow • strong alignment with trap
hi‑hat patterns

Common genres:

• trap • modern hip‑hop • melodic rap

  ------------------------
  4. GRIME SWING CADENCE
  ------------------------

Structure:

DA — da DA — da

Example structure:

Step in the rave with a mic and a vision Everyone knows that the flow’s
precision

Characteristics:

• slight delay behind the beat • syncopated rhythm • aggressive
consonant attack

Common genres:

• UK grime • UK drill • fast rap flows

  --------------------------
  5. MELODIC GLIDE CADENCE
  --------------------------

Structure:

Long vowel phrasing with flowing syllables.

Example structure:

Baby don’t leave me alone tonight

Characteristics:

• sustained vowels • smooth melodic transitions • emotional vocal
delivery

Common genres:

• R&B • pop ballads • melodic rap

  ----------------------
  6. PUNCHLINE CADENCE
  ----------------------

Structure:

short short short LONG

Example structure:

You lied You ran You broke Now watch me burn

Characteristics:

• tension build across lines • strong final emphasis • clear lyrical
impact

Common genres:

• battle rap • aggressive rap • heavy music lyrics

  ----------------------------
  7. BREAKDOWN CHANT CADENCE
  ----------------------------

Structure:

WORD WORD WORD WORD DROP PHRASE

Example structure:

Break the chain Burn the throne KILL THEM ALL

Characteristics:

• extremely simple rhythm • chantable phrases • designed for loud mixes

Common genres:

• deathcore • metalcore • hardcore

  ---------------------
  8. POP HOOK CADENCE
  ---------------------

Structure:

DA da DA da DA da DA da

Example structure:

If you wanna love me You gotta treat me right

Characteristics:

• simple rhythm • chantable delivery • immediate memorability

Common genres:

• commercial pop • dance pop • radio hooks

  -------------------------
  9. STORYTELLING CADENCE
  -------------------------

Structure:

Natural speech rhythm.

Example structure:

I remember when you said you’d never leave

Characteristics:

• conversational delivery • flexible rhythm • narrative clarity

Common genres:

• storytelling rap • singer‑songwriter • folk

  --------------------
  10. HYBRID CADENCE
  --------------------

Structure:

Multiple cadence shifts inside one verse.

Example pattern:

double‑time double‑time pause punchline

Example structure:

Running through the night with the weight of the city Everybody talking
but they never really with me … Now I own the crown

Characteristics:

• dynamic rhythm • dramatic pacing • advanced performance technique

Common genres:

• modern hip‑hop • progressive rap • concept tracks

  ------------------------
  CADENCE SELECTION RULE
  ------------------------

Choose cadence based on emotion and genre.

Aggression → punchline or double‑time Sadness → melodic glide Narrative
→ storytelling cadence Hype → chant cadence Trap energy → triplet
cadence

  --------------------------
  CADENCE VALIDATION CHECK
  --------------------------

Before finalizing lyrics:

1.  Speak the line aloud.
2.  Identify stressed syllables.
3.  Align stresses with beat accents.
4.  Remove awkward syllables.

If the line cannot be spoken naturally in rhythm, rewrite it.

  ----------------------
  WORKFLOW INTEGRATION
  ----------------------

Cadence selection occurs after phonetic texture but before rhyme
optimization.

System order:

Story Rule ↓ Sentence Rule ↓ Phonetic Texture Rule ↓ Cadence Pattern
Library ↓ Rhyme–Cadence Interaction ↓ Genre Pattern ↓ Final Validation ↓
Production Prompt

  ----------
  KEY RULE
  ----------

Cadence determines how lyrics feel when performed.

A technically strong lyric that cannot be delivered rhythmically is
still a failed lyric.
