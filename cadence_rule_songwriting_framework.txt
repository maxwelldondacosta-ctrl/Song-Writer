THE CADENCE RULE Song Writing Project – Delivery and Rhythm Framework

  ----------
  OVERVIEW
  ----------

The Cadence Rule governs how lyrics are rhythmically delivered when
performed. While the Sentence Rule ensures meaning and the Story Rule
ensures narrative impact, cadence determines how the words actually ride
the beat and vocal delivery.

Cadence is created by the interaction between:

• syllable length • rhyme placement • phonetic texture • emotional tone
• lyrical topic

Writers must imagine how a line will be performed while writing it.

  ----------------
  CORE PRINCIPLE
  ----------------

Lyrics should be written with an imagined delivery rhythm.

If a line cannot be naturally spoken or performed within the intended
musical rhythm, it should be revised.

  --------------------
  CADENCE COMPONENTS
  --------------------

1.  BEAT FIT

A lyric line must sit naturally within the rhythm of the song.

Shorter phrases often work better in aggressive or fast genres, while
longer flowing lines suit slower emotional music.

Example punch cadence:

Cold night Street light Mind tight

These segments allow rapid rhythmic delivery.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.  STRESS PATTERN

The placement of stressed syllables determines the impact of the lyric.

Important words should land on rhythmic accents.

Example:

BREAK the chain FEEL the pain

Strong syllables fall on the beat, increasing emotional power.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.  EMOTIONAL DELIVERY

Cadence must match the emotional tone of the song.

Emotion → Typical cadence style

Anger / aggression: Short punch phrases, hard stops.

Sadness / reflection: Slower flowing lines, longer vowels.

Romance / tenderness: Soft consonants and smoother transitions.

  --------------------------
  CADENCE CONTRAST EXAMPLE
  --------------------------

Same topic: love

Soft romantic cadence:

I love you baby Stay close tonight Hold me forever Everything’s right

Characteristics: • longer vowels • softer consonants • smooth rhythm

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Raw physical cadence:

Pull you close Hands on skin Lose control Let me in

Characteristics: • shorter words • harder consonants • rhythmic punch

  ---------------------
  WHY CADENCE MATTERS
  ---------------------

Two lyrics may express the same meaning but feel completely different
because of cadence.

Cadence determines:

• vocal rhythm • emotional intensity • audience engagement •
memorability

  ------------------------------
  INTEGRATION WITH OTHER RULES
  ------------------------------

Sentence Rule: Ensures the lyric makes logical sense.

Story Rule: Ensures the song communicates a narrative arc.

Phonetic Texture Rule: Ensures word sounds match emotional tone.

Cadence Rule: Ensures the lyric flows rhythmically when performed.

Pattern Libraries: Ensure the lyric structure fits the genre.

  ----------
  KEY RULE
  ----------

Write lyrics as if they are already being performed.

If the line cannot be delivered naturally with rhythm and emotional
emphasis, it must be revised.
