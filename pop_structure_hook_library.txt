  ---------
  PURPOSE 
  ---------

This document defines structural templates, lyrical frameworks,
vocabulary hints, and hook psychology principles used in modern
commercial pop songwriting.

Pop differs from other genres in a key way:

The hook must be instantly memorable.

Because of this, pop songwriting prioritizes:

• simple language • strong repetition • clear emotional situations •
melodic phrasing • highly memorable hooks

  ----------------------
  THE POP PARADOX RULE
  ----------------------

Pop lyrics should express adult situations using simple, almost
childlike language.

The goal is not poetic complexity but memorability.

Example structure:

Adult situation: relationship expectations

Pop translation: “If you wanna love me you gotta treat me right”

Listeners should understand and remember the hook after one listen.

  ------------------------------
  CHILDLIKE LANGUAGE PRINCIPLE
  ------------------------------

The most successful pop songs often sound like playground chants about
adult emotions.

Hooks frequently use:

• simple verbs • everyday vocabulary • repetitive phrasing

Example hook pattern:

If you wanna [action] You gotta [rule]

This creates a memorable rhythmic chant.

  -------------------------
  VERSE WRITING FRAMEWORK
  -------------------------

Line 1 – Situation Line 2 – Emotional reaction Line 3 – Consequence Line
4 – Personal feeling

Example:

You walked out the door Didn’t say goodbye Now every song we loved Makes
me cry

  ----------------------
  PRE-CHORUS FRAMEWORK
  ----------------------

The pre-chorus builds tension into the hook.

Structure:

Realization Emotional escalation Short repeating phrase

Example:

Every time you say my name I feel the same I feel the same

  -----------------------
  CHORUS HOOK FRAMEWORK
  -----------------------

A pop chorus must contain a clear memorable phrase.

Hook pattern:

Hook line Hook line repeat Variation line Final hook

Example:

I can’t let you go I can’t let you go Every road leads back to you I
can’t let you go

  ----------------
  BRIDGE PURPOSE
  ----------------

The bridge introduces a twist or confession.

Possible functions:

• emotional realization • vulnerability • confession of love • regret

Example:

Maybe I was wrong Maybe I was blind But every time I run You pull me
back inside

  ---------------------
  POP VOCABULARY BANK
  ---------------------

Pop favors words that are:

• simple • melodic • emotionally direct

  ----------------
  LOVE / ROMANCE
  ----------------

love heart baby kiss touch hold mine yours forever together

  ---------
  LONGING
  ---------

miss need want dream stay call remember wait alone belong

  ----------------------
  CELEBRATION / ENERGY
  ----------------------

dance tonight lights shine alive free wild fire glow run

  ------------
  HEARTBREAK
  ------------

goodbye broken tears lonely empty lost gone regret fading over

  ----------------
  HOOK WORD RULE
  ----------------

Pop hooks should favor short memorable words such as:

love baby night fire heart home stay mine

Complex vocabulary weakens hook memorability.

  --------------------------
  SONG GENERATION WORKFLOW
  --------------------------

1.  Choose pop structure template

2.  Choose topic (love, heartbreak, longing, celebration, regret)

3.  Apply Story Rule (event → emotion → resolution)

4.  Apply Sentence Rule validation

5.  Select vocabulary from pop vocabulary bank

6.  Shape melodic cadence

7.  Build hook phrase

8.  Generate Suno prompt

  ----------------
  CORE PRINCIPLE
  ----------------

If the hook cannot be remembered after one listen, it is too complex for
pop.
